swp

IPW User Command
Category - Environmental Physics and Properties: Point

NAME

swp - soil water potential (SWP) and hydaulic conductivities

SYNOPSIS

swp

DESCRIPTION

swp is one of a group of commands designed to simulate energy, climate, or hydrologic processes or properties at a point. Most were originally written as "qdips" (Quick and Dirty Image Processing System) programs by D. Marks, during the 1980's at UCSB.

swp calculates soil water potential (SWP), and saturated and unsaturated hydraulic conductivity from %sand, %clay, and % soil water content (SWC) by volume. Outputs SWP in kPa and hyd. cond in both m/s and cm/hr, saturated SWP and hydraulic conductivity, and water content ((m^3 h2o)/(m^3 of soil))*100 at 10 kPa, and at saturation. (after Saxton, et al, Soil Science Society of America Journal, Vol. 50, pp. 1031-1036, 1986)

OPTIONS

none

OPERANDS

none

EXAMPLES

To calculate SWP, saturated and unsaturated hydraulic conductivity from the following inputs: 

     %sand   %clay   SWC
     65      10      0.02
     65      10      0.10
     65      10      0.20
     65      10      0.30
     65      10      0.40

        paste sand clay SWC | swp
